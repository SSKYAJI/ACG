Looking at the code, the issue is in the `Email.__call__` method. When the domain part fails the `DOMAIN_REGEX` match, it tries to encode it as IDNA. However, the IDNA encoding itself doesn't validate that labels don't start or end with hyphens - that check needs to happen after encoding.

Let me trace through what happens with `user@-münchen.de`:
1. `domain_part = "-münchen.de"` fails `DOMAIN_REGEX` (starts with hyphen)
2. It tries `domain_part.encode("idna")` - Python's `idna` codec may or may not raise for leading hyphens
3. If encoding succeeds, it checks `DOMAIN_REGEX.match(domain_part)` on the ASCII result

The fix is to validate that after IDNA encoding, the resulting domain labels don't start or end with hyphens.
