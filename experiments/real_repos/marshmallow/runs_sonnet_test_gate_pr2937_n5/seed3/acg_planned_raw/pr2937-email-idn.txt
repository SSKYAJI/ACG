Looking at the code, the issue is in the `Email.__call__` method. When the domain part fails the `DOMAIN_REGEX` match, it tries to encode it with IDNA encoding. However, the IDNA encoding itself doesn't validate that labels don't start or end with hyphens - it just converts the unicode to punycode. The fix should validate the encoded domain labels to ensure none start or end with a hyphen.

Let me trace through the logic:
1. `user@-münchen.de` - domain is `-münchen.de`
2. `DOMAIN_REGEX` won't match (starts with hyphen)
3. It tries `domain_part.encode("idna")` - this might succeed or fail
4. If it succeeds, it checks `DOMAIN_REGEX.match(domain_part)` on the encoded version

The fix: after encoding to IDNA, we should also validate that no label in the encoded domain starts or ends with a hyphen.
