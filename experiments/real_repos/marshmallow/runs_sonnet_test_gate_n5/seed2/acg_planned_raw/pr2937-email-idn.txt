Looking at the code, the issue is in the `Email.__call__` method. When an IDN domain is processed, it's encoded using `.encode("idna")` and then validated against `DOMAIN_REGEX`. However, the IDNA encoding itself doesn't reject labels starting or ending with hyphens - we need to check for that before or after encoding.

The fix should validate that no domain label starts or ends with a hyphen after IDNA encoding (or before, by checking the encoded form).

Let me trace through the logic:
- `user@-münchen.de` → domain is `-münchen.de`
- IDNA encoding of `-münchen.de` → this might actually fail or produce `-xn--mnchen-3ya.de`
- We need to check that encoded labels don't start/end with hyphens

The fix: after encoding the domain to IDNA ASCII, check that no label starts or ends with a hyphen before matching against `DOMAIN_REGEX`.
