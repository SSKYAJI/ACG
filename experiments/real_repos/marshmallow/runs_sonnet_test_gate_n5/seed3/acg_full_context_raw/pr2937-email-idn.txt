Looking at the code, the issue is in the `Email.__call__` method. When an IDN domain is encoded via `domain_part.encode("idna").decode("ascii")`, the resulting punycode is then validated against `DOMAIN_REGEX`. However, the `encode("idna")` call doesn't raise a `UnicodeError` for labels starting or ending with hyphens in all Python versions - it may succeed and produce something like `xn---mnchen-3ya` which could pass the regex even though the original label starts/ends with a hyphen.

The fix is to check each label of the domain for leading/trailing hyphens before attempting IDNA encoding.
