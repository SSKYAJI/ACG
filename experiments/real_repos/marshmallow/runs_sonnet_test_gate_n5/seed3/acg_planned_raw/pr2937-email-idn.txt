Looking at the code, the issue is in the `Email.__call__` method. When an IDN domain like `-münchen.de` or `münchen-.de` is processed, it gets encoded via `.encode("idna")` and then checked against `DOMAIN_REGEX`. However, the `encode("idna")` call may succeed for some malformed labels (or the regex may not catch them), allowing invalid emails through.

The fix should validate each label of the domain after IDNA encoding to ensure no label starts or ends with a hyphen.

The safest fix is to explicitly check each domain label after IDNA encoding to ensure none starts or ends with a hyphen.
